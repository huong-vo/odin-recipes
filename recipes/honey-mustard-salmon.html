Honey Mustard Salmon

Description

This simple honey mustard salmon recipe is inspired by the recipe Ana Ankeny from The Real Food Dieticians. It is a flavorful sheet pan meal that potentially portions into up to 4 servings. It be quickly prepared within 15 minutes and is ready to eat in just another 15-18 minutes.

The dish is highly nutritious with a good amount of protein, fiber, carbs, vitamins, and minerals. It is an inviting meal with vibrant colors, a sweet taste from honey, a tangy taste from mustard, and a savory taste from other seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound salmon fillet, cut into 4 portions
  • 1-2 pounds asparagus
  • 1 pound small red or yellow potatoes, cut in half if large
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 3 tablespoons mustard
  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon, after zesting squeeze the lemon for 3 teaspoons lemon juice and cut the rest into wedges for serving
  • 2 teaspoons finely minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoons grated ginger
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¼ teaspoon fine salt
  • Cooking spray or cooking oil

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, mustard, 1 tablespoon oil, lemon zest, 2 teaspoons lemon juice, garlic, ginger, salt, and thyme until smooth. Set aside 3 tablespoons of the sauce for tossing with the potatoes.
  3. Place the salmon fillets in a shallow bowl. Spoon the remaining honey mustard mixture over top and use a marinade brush to coat the salmon on all sides. Set aside.
  4. Grease the baking sheet pan with cooking spray or oil. Place the potatoes on the prepared baking sheet. Toss the potatoes with the 3 tablespoons reserved honey mustard until well coated. Spread the potatoes, cut-side down in an even layer on the pan. Bake for 15 minutes.
  5. Remove the pan from the oven and push the potatoes to one side so they take up about ⅓ the pan.
  6. Add the asparagus to the opposite end of the pan leaving room for the salmon in the middle.
  7. Drizzle the asparagus with the remaining ½ tablespoon oil and toss until evenly coated. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
  8. Place the salmon on the baking sheet between the potatoes and asparagus. Cook until the salmon flakes easily with a fork, another 8-13 minutes, depending on the thickness.
  9. Serve with lemon wedges and 1 teaspoon lemon juice.
