Honey Mustard Salmon

Description

This simple honey mustard salmon recipe is inspired by the recipe Ana Ankeny from The Real Food Dieticians. It is a flavorful sheet pan meal that potentially portions into up to 4 servings. It be quickly prepared within 15 minutes and is ready to eat in just another 15-18 minutes.

The dish is highly nutritious with a good amount of protein, fiber, carbs, vitamins, and minerals. It is an inviting meal with vibrant colors, a sweet taste from honey, a tangy taste from mustard, and a savory taste from other seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound salmon fillet, cut into 4 portions
  • 1-2 pounds asparagus
  • 1 pound small potatoes cut in half
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons mustard
  • 1¼ tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon, after zesting squeeze the lemon for 3 teaspoons lemon juice and cut the rest into wedges for serving
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoons grated ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon fine salt
  • Cooking spray or cooking oil

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Place the potatoes into a large bowl.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, mustard, 1 tablespoon oil, lemon zest, 2 teaspoons lemon juice, garlic, ginger, and salt until smooth. Pour 3 tablespoons of the sauce into the potato bowl. Set aside.
  4. Place the salmon fillets in a shallow bowl. Pour the remaining sauce on top and use a basting brush to coat the salmon on all sides. Set aside.
  5. Grease the baking sheet pan with cooking spray or oil. Toss the potatoes with the sauce until well coated. Spread the potatoes with cut-side down on the pan. Bake for 15 minutes.
  6. Remove the pan from the oven and push the potatoes to one side.
  7. Add the asparagus to the opposite end of the pan, leaving the middle for the salmon later.
  8. Drizzle the asparagus with the remaining ¼ tablespoon oil and toss until evenly coated.
  9. Place the salmon between the potatoes and asparagus. Cook until the salmon for 12 minutes. Once done, take it out of the oven.
  10. Drizzle the remaining lemon juice on top of the salmon fillets.
  11. Serve with lemon wedges and enjoy!
